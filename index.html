About me

I am an Associate Computational Biologist based in Boston, Massachusetts. After conducting vascular biology research at the Boston University Medical Campus, I now work at Harvard Medical School, focusing on immunology and the systems-level exploration of the immune response.

Rather than examining individual genes or molecules in isolation, my work emphasizes understanding the immune system as a coordinated network. We ask: How do genes, pathways, cell types, and signaling mechanisms interact to drive complex biological responses?

My systems-level approach involves:

  • Studying cytokine and chemokine signaling networks
  • Investigating cell-type interactions using single-cell and spatial transcriptomics
  • Modeling immune cell coordination and communication across tissues

A core aspect of my role is the development and optimization of robust bioinformatics pipelines using workflow management systems such as Nextflow, CWL, WDL, and Snakemake. I work extensively with R, Python, and Linux, including:

  • Loading .RDS and .RData files containing Seurat objects in R
  • Analyzing .h5ad (AnnData) files in Python for exploratory single-cell analysis

Currently, I am expanding my skill set to incorporate machine learning techniques specifically tailored for biological datasets. My goal is to improve pattern recognition, enable predictive modeling, and generate new hypotheses from complex biological data.

At the heart of my work lies a passion for problem-solving, cross-disciplinary collaboration, and contributing to the advancement of biomedical science through data-driven discovery.

What i'm doing

  • Bulk RNA Sequencing

    Bulk RNA sequencing analyzes the collective gene expression of a sample, offering insights into overall gene activity and differences between conditions.

  • Web development

    I know how to use HTML,CSS, RShiny, SQL, CGI, and Javascript to create websites. Previously I have created a Transposon database.

  • scRNA Sequencing

    I analyze scRNA sequencing data. Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) is a cutting-edge technique that allows researchers to analyze the gene expression profile of individual cells within a complex sample, providing insights into cellular heterogeneity and dynamics at a single-cell resolution.

  • Spatial Transcriptomics

    Spatial transcriptomics combines gene expression data with the spatial location of cells in a tissue, allowing researchers to map where specific genes are active within their native biological context.
